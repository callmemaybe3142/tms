### Contents of this page
Vision
To be a learning center of excellence in medical education that produces competent, ethical, and socially accountable medical doctors dedicated to serving the health needs of the people of Tealand and the wider federal union.

Mission
- Quality Education: To provide high-quality medical education aligned with international standards and national interim frameworks.
- Healthcare Solutions: To address the critical shortage of healthcare professionals in underserved and conflict-affected regions.
- Social Impact: To foster a learning environment that emphasizes professional integrity, human rights, and social justice.

Core Values
Excellence: A steadfast commitment to high academic and clinical standards.
Integrity: Strict adherence to ethical, medical, and academic practices.
Equity: Ensuring non-discriminatory access to education and healthcare for all.
Accountability: Maintaining a deep responsibility to the community and governing authorities.

Governance & Leadership
Tealand Medical School operates under the Ta'angland Health Council (THC). Strategic decisions are managed by the TMS Governing Board, while academic integrity is ensured through our affiliation with IUC-UMM.

Organizational Structure
Executive Leadership: Rector / Principal
Academic Wing: Academic Council, Vice-Principal (Academic), and Heads of Pre-Clinical, Para-Clinical, and Clinical Departments.
Administrative Wing: Vice-Principal (Admin & Ops), Finance, HR, and Security Units

## Academics Page
### Contents of this page
Tealand Medical School (TMS) offers a rigorous 6-year M.B,B.S. program designed to produce medical professionals who are technically competent and socially responsible. Our curriculum follows the modern Integrated Medical Education model, blending theoretical knowledge with early clinical exposure.

1. Curriculum Structure
The curriculum is divided into three distinct phases to ensure a smooth transition from basic sciences to advanced clinical practice.

2. Teaching & Learning Methodology
We utilize a Blended Learning approach to ensure education remains accessible and high-quality even in challenging environments:
Theoretical Modules: Delivered through advanced e-learning platforms and virtual classrooms.
Clinical Training: Conducted face-to-face at health facilities authorized by the Ta'angland Health Council (THC).
Integrated Learning: Bridging the gap between foundational sciences and clinical application from day one.

3. Assessment & Quality Assurance
To maintain academic integrity, TMS employs a dual assessment strategy:
Formative Assessment: Continuous evaluation through quizzes, clinical observations, and feedback.
Summative Assessment: Professional examinations conducted in strict coordination with the Interim University Council, University of Medicine, Mandalay (IUC-UMM) standards.

### Contents of this page
Tealand Medical School seeks dedicated students who are committed to serving their communities. Our admission policy is built on the principles of transparency, merit, and social equity.

1. Selection Criteria
Admission to the M.B,B.S. program is highly competitive. Candidates are selected based on:
Academic Merit: Previous educational performance in science-related subjects.
Entrance Evaluation: Success in the TMS entrance assessment process.
Personal Integrity: A demonstrated commitment to medical ethics and community service.

2. Priority & Equity Policy
In line with our mission to address healthcare shortages in underserved regions, special consideration and priority are given to:
Candidates hailing from Tealand.
Students from underserved ethnic communities across the federal union.

3. Institutional Recognition & Student Mobility
TMS ensures that our students’ education is recognized within the national and regional healthcare framework:
Official Awarding: The M.B,B.S. degree is officially conferred by the Interim University Council, University of Medicine, Mandalay (IUC-UMM).
Accreditation: TMS is institutionally registered with the NUG Ministry of Education (MOE) and operates in policy coordination with the NUG Ministry of Health (MOH).
Credit Transfers: Through the NUG/EAO health network, we facilitate credit transfers and clinical placement rotations at various partner hospitals, ensuring student mobility.
